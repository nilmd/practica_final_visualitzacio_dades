# Product Vision

This project should feel like a modern editorial data experience rather than a traditional analytics dashboard.

Think:

* Apple product pages
* Airbnb design language
* Stripe storytelling
* Our World in Data visual narratives

The experience should be:

* Clean
* Elegant
* Focused
* Data-driven
* Minimalist

Avoid clutter.

Avoid showing too many charts at once.

Every visualization must answer a specific question.

The user should progressively discover how electricity is generated around the world.

---

# Core Story

The project answers one fundamental question:

**Who generates the world's electricity?**

The narrative should guide users through three progressively deeper insights:

1. What powers the world?
2. How do countries differ?
3. Which countries have the most resilient and diversified energy systems?

Every section should contribute to answering these questions.

---

# Design Principles

## Simplicity First

Prioritize clarity over quantity.

Do not create a dashboard full of widgets.

Each section should contain:

* One key question
* One main visualization
* One supporting insight

Nothing more.

## Visual Hierarchy

Large typography.

Generous whitespace.

Minimal color palette.

Use color only when it communicates data.

Avoid decorative elements.

## Story Before Exploration

Users should first understand the story.

Only then should they explore the data.

Each section should begin with a narrative statement.

Example:

"Although renewable energy is rapidly expanding, fossil fuels still dominate global electricity infrastructure."

Then reveal the visualization.

---

# User Journey

## Section 1 — Introduction

Question:

What does the global electricity system look like?

Display:

Large KPI cards.

Minimal animation.

Key metrics:

* Power plants
* Countries
* Installed capacity
* Energy sources

The goal is to establish scale.

---

## Section 2 — What Powers the World?

Question:

Which energy sources dominate global electricity generation?

Visualization:

Single treemap.

Purpose:

Show relative importance of each technology.

Supporting text should explain the main takeaway.

The user should immediately understand that not all technologies contribute equally.

---

## Section 3 — Geography of Electricity

Question:

Where are these infrastructures located?

Visualization:

Interactive global map.

This should be the visual centerpiece of the project.

Interaction:

* Zoom
* Pan
* Hover

Filters:

* Fuel type
* Capacity range

Avoid excessive controls.

Focus on discovery.

---

## Section 4 — Different Countries, Different Strategies

Question:

Do countries generate electricity in the same way?

Visualization:

Country comparison tool.

User selects two countries.

Show:

* Capacity distribution by fuel type
* Renewable share
* Total installed capacity

The goal is comparison, not exploration.

Keep the interface extremely simple.

---

## Section 5 — Energy Diversity

Question:

Which countries are most dependent on a single technology?

Visualization:

Diversification chart.

Purpose:

Reveal that some countries rely heavily on one energy source while others maintain a balanced portfolio.

This section should introduce the Diversification Index.

Provide a simple explanation.

Avoid statistical jargon.

---

## Section 6 — Key Findings

Final storytelling section.

No charts.

Only insights.

Examples:

* Most diversified country
* Largest renewable share
* Largest coal dependency
* Largest power plant
* Most common technology

Present them as elegant insight cards.

The project should end with conclusions, not with another visualization.

---

# Dashboard Philosophy

The dashboard is not the product.

The story is the product.

The dashboard exists only to support the story.

Every chart must justify its existence.

If a visualization does not answer one of the core research questions, remove it.

Quality is preferred over quantity.

A small number of excellent visualizations is better than many average ones.

---

# Desired Emotional Outcome

At the end of the experience, users should feel that they have understood:

* How electricity is generated globally.
* How different countries approach energy production.
* Why energy diversification matters.
* The scale and complexity of the world's electricity infrastructure.

The experience should feel informative, modern and professional.
